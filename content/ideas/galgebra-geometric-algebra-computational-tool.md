For computation exercises within the book [Linear and Geometric Algebra by Alan Macdonald](https://www.faculty.luther.edu/~macdonal/laga/),
the best choose of tools are:

* For Geometric Algebra symbolic mathematics computation, [GAlgebra](https://galgebra.readthedocs.io/en/latest/) is a community fork at
[GitHub: pygae/galgebra](https://github.com/pygae/galgebra) and maintained by [Pythonic Geometric Algebra Enthusiasts](https://github.com/pygae)
* For graphics visualization, you can use [GitHub: pygae/pyganja](https://github.com/pygae/pyganja).
If you are calling its API from a script it will render them in a [`cefpython`](https://github.com/cztomczak/cefpython) window
or if you are in a Jupyter Notebooks it can simply render in the notebook itself.

These are not the only Geometric Algebra computation tools you could use.
[Geometric Algebra Explorer](https://ga-explorer.netlify.app/index.php/ga-software/) contains a list of many tools.

# Design & Animate Robotics Mechanisms 
[Kinematics Summer School (KiSS)](https://sites.google.com/stonybrook.edu/2019kiss/videos)

GIM is a registered general purpose software for the kinematics and dynamics analysis of planar and spatial mechanisms,
created by the COMPMECH research group in the University of the Basque Country UPV/EHU. 
* [MotionGen](https://motiongen.io/)
* [GIM](https://www.ehu.eus/compmech/software/)
  * [Tutorial of GIM Software](https://www.ehu.eus/compmech/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/GIM_Tutorial.pdf)
  * [GIM software by Compmech ResearchGroup](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLiVwFLfy3yfDSggGnerNF3NKHYwBsxHVs)
